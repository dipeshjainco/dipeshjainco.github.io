Services

All companies incorporated in India are required to comply with various provisions of the Companies Act, 2013 and the rules made there under. All limited liability partnerships incorporated in India are required to comply with various provisions of the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008 and the rules made there under. Companies are required to obtain various licenses, certificates and approvals from several regulatory authorities. We advise companies on several issues arising in corporate and allied laws. Our services include the following.

INCORPORATION

Advisory service regarding the most suitable form of organization, based on the clients specific needs and objectives; incorporation of all types of companies under the Companies Act, 2013, Limited Liability Partnerships, Wholly Owned Subsidiaries.

Obtaining all required approvals and licenses for commencement of the business from the Registrar of Companies, Ministry of Corporate Affairs.

SECRETARIAL SERVICES

Regular Compliance and Advisory services [on both ongoing and retainer basis] concerning Company Law and law relating to LLP’s.

REGULATORY APPROVALS & REPRESENTATION

A corporate organization needs to obtain various approvals from time to time from various government and quasi- government bodies under various laws and regulations. We help clients to obtain the various regulatory approvals. We also represent clients before various authorities such as Ministry of Corporate Affairs, Stamp Authorities, to name a few.

AUDIT

Corporate Compliance Management (CCM) ensures organizational efficiency, transparency as well as investor confidence. Ever evolving laws and regulations have placed immense responsibilities on Directors and Managerial Personnel of Companies who must protect the interest of all stakeholders.Thus, audit of records and documents by an external professional services firm is vital in ensuring that all required compliances are in place and in seeking advice on the corrective measures that need to be taken, in case discrepancies or incidents of non-compliance are noted.

Secretarial Audit: The Companies Act, 2013 has introduced Secretarial Audit as a new class of audit which is mandatory for every listed company and companies having a paid up capital of Rs. 50 Crores or more, or a turnover of Rs. 250 Crores and more, or as prescribed.The scope of audit covers compliance to all the laws applicable to the corporate in addition to the Companies Act, 2013, SEBI and other Securities laws, Foreign Exchange Management Act, Labour Laws as well as the Secretarial Standards issued by ICSI.

We adhere to the highest standards of professional ethics while undertaking the Audit to abide by the spirit of the law.
